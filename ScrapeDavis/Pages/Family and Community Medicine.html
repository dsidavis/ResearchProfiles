Family Medicine
at UC Davis

UC Davis family physicians provide medical care that is
leading-edge, culturally sensitive, evidence based and reflects the ideals of the medical home.

Feature Stories

Prior studies found that diabetics with normal BMIs have a higher mortality risk than those who are overweight or obese.“The mortality paradox led to the ‘protection explanation’ that seems unlikely to be true because we didn’t see any beneficial effects of excess weight in people who did not have diabetes,” said Anthony Jerant, professor of family and community medicine at UC Davis and lead author of the studies.  Read more in the UC Davis Health System News

UC Davis primary care practices receive prestigious recognition
"Achieving the Patient-Centered Medical Home designation reflects the many ways that UC Davis emphasizes the primacy of our patients in the delivery of primary care," said Thomas Balsbaugh, medical director for the Patient-Centered Medical Home and care coordination at UC Davis. "We provide a multi-disciplinary team to patients and families, and offer culturally relevant, proactive care". Read more in the UC Davis Health System News

The specialty of family medicine is centered on lasting, caring relationships with patients and their families. Family physicians integrate the biological, clinical and behavioral sciences to provide continuing and comprehensive health care. The scope of family medicine encompasses all ages, sexes, each organ system and every disease entity.
